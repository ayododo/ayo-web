# Art Directions — pick one, vary between projects

The fastest way for a skill to become a template is producing the same look twice. Choose the direction that fits the project's audience and energy — and when two projects are similar, deliberately pick differently. Never default to Night Broadcast out of habit.

## 1. Bold & Friendly (the Wise lesson)

For community clubs, grassroots sport, youth academies, fan-facing consumer apps, ticketing.
Wise.com proves a bold identity needs almost no motion — the boldness lives in type and color.

- **Color**: one high-contrast duo owned completely — bright green #9FE870-class on forest #163300-class (or bright yellow/deep navy, coral/oxblood). Light warm-neutral base; full-bleed color-block sections in the deep tone with the bright tone as giant text on top. Bright-on-deep IS the identity.
- **Type**: chunky, rounded, slightly quirky display face (Wise Sans energy — try Bricolage Grotesque, Schibsted Grotesk, or Hanken Grotesk at 800) at enormous sizes, tight line-height. Sentence case, not all-caps — friendly, not shouty.
- **Shapes**: pill buttons, generously rounded cards (16–24px), circular badges. No hard edges.
- **The functional widget**: Wise's hero is a working calculator. Sport equivalent: a match ticket picker, membership price selector, or countdown — a real interactive tool in the hero beats any animation.
- **Motion**: almost none. Hover states, one gentle reveal, that's it.

## 2. Night Broadcast

For big-club matchday, live scores, esports, events. Dark stadium-night base, one electric accent (volt/tangerine/cyan) + calm deep pair, condensed caps display (Anton-class), mono data, broadcast grammar (tickers, LIVE badges, score bugs). Full detail: `matchday.md`. This is the loudest direction — reserve it for subjects that are actually loud.

## 3. Club Heritage

For clubs with history, retro kits, anniversary pages, supporter culture.
- **Color**: cream/paper base + one deep club color (bottle green, maroon, navy) + a thin gold or contrast stripe. Feels like a 1970s matchday programme.
- **Type**: a strong serif or slab for headlines (crest energy), grotesk for UI; jersey-number figures as decorative elements.
- **Texture**: subtle paper grain, hairline double-rules, stamp/crest shapes, vertical stripes from kit patterns.
- **Motion**: minimal — this direction sells permanence, not speed.

## 4. Performance Premium

For premium gear brands, athlete portfolios, training platforms. Restrained palette to the last pixel, monospace precision, editorial imagery, product-centered. Full detail: `performance-premium.md`.

## Choosing rules

- Community/family audience → Bold & Friendly. Big-match adrenaline → Night Broadcast. Tradition/pride → Club Heritage. Aspirational/technical → Performance Premium.
- State the chosen direction in the concept sentence (Step 2) before writing code.
- Whichever direction: numbers stay oversized, content stays specific, and the one-sentence identity test still applies.
